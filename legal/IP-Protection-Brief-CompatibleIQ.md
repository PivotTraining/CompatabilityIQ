## 1. Trademark Protection

### What to Trademark

You have four primary trademark candidates, in order of priority:

1. **"CompatibleIQ"** -- The core product name. This is the highest-priority filing. File as a standard character mark (protecting the word itself regardless of font/style) and optionally as a stylized mark if you have a finalized logo.

2. **"CIQ"** -- The abbreviation. Consider filing separately if you intend to use "CIQ" as a standalone brand element (in the app UI, marketing, domain name). Short marks can be harder to register because the USPTO may find them descriptive or likely to cause confusion with existing marks. An attorney can advise whether this is worth a separate filing or if protection under the "CompatibleIQ" mark is sufficient.

3. **The Logo** -- File as a design mark once the logo is finalized. A design mark protects the specific visual representation. Do not file until the design is final -- any significant change after registration would require a new filing.

4. **"Date with proof."** -- The tagline. Taglines can be registered as trademarks. This one is strong because it is short, distinctive, and not merely descriptive. File as a standard character mark.

### How to File

- **Filing system:** USPTO TEAS Plus (Trademark Electronic Application System Plus) -- the lowest-cost online filing option at $250 per class per mark.
- **TEAS Plus requirements:** You must select goods/services descriptions from the USPTO's pre-approved Acceptable Identification of Goods and Services Manual. Custom descriptions require TEAS Standard ($350/class/mark).
- **Filing basis:** Use "Intent to Use" (Section 1b) if the product is not yet in commerce. You can convert to "Use in Commerce" once launched. Intent-to-Use requires a Statement of Use filing later ($100/class) and you may need extensions ($125 each, up to 5 extensions for 3 additional years total).

### International Classes to File Under

File under the following Nice Classification classes:

| Class | Description | Why |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| **Class 9** | Downloadable mobile application software for dating, matchmaking, and compatibility assessment | Covers the iOS/Android app itself |
| **Class 42** | Software as a service (SaaS) featuring software for dating, matchmaking, personality assessment, and compatibility scoring | Covers the web platform and SaaS aspects |
| **Class 45** | Online dating services; matchmaking services; compatibility assessment services for interpersonal relationships | Covers the dating/matchmaking service itself |
| **Class 41** | Educational services in the field of personal development and relationship skills (optional, for Pivot Growth Track) | Covers the coaching/training component |

**Minimum recommended filing:** Classes 9, 42, and 45 for "CompatibleIQ." Add Class 41 if Pivot Growth Track is a significant revenue line.

### Timeline

- **USPTO examination:** 8-12 months from filing to first Office Action or approval
- **Publication for opposition:** 30-day window after approval where third parties can object
- **Registration:** If no opposition, registration issues approximately 3 months after publication
- **Total timeline:** 12-18 months from filing to registration (assuming no complications)
- **Intent-to-Use addition:** You must file a Statement of Use within 6 months of Notice of Allowance (extendable up to 3 years with extension requests)

### TM Symbol: Use It Now

- **Use TM immediately.** You do not need a federal registration to use the TM symbol. It signals that you claim trademark rights in the name. Start using "CompatibleIQ(TM)" and "Date with proof.(TM)" in all marketing materials, the app, and the website right now.
- **Do not use the (R) symbol** until you receive a federal registration certificate. Using (R) without registration is illegal and can jeopardize your application.

### Search Recommendations Before Filing

Before spending money on filings, conduct a trademark clearance search:

1. **Free preliminary search:** Search the USPTO's Trademark Electronic Search System (TESS) at tess2.uspto.gov for "CompatibleIQ," "Compatible IQ," "CIQ," and similar marks in Classes 9, 42, and 45.
2. **Google and app store search:** Search Google, Apple App Store, and Google Play for any existing dating/assessment apps using "CompatibleIQ" or confusingly similar names.
3. **Domain search:** Check domain registrations for compatibleiq.com and variations.
4. **Professional comprehensive search:** Before filing, strongly consider paying $500-$1,500 for a comprehensive search through a trademark search firm (e.g., Corsearch, TrademarkNow). This searches federal, state, common law, domain, and international databases. An attorney can order this for you and provide a legal opinion on registrability.

## 2. Copyright Protection

### What Is Automatically Copyrighted

Under U.S. law, copyright attaches automatically upon creation of an original work fixed in a tangible medium. The following CompatibleIQ assets are already protected by copyright:

- **Source code** -- All Next.js, React, TypeScript, and Edge Function code
- **Assessment question bank** -- The 122 questions across 6 modules (the specific expression of the questions, not the underlying psychological concepts)
- **UI/UX design** -- Screen layouts, visual design elements, interaction flows
- **Marketing copy** -- Website text, app store descriptions, taglines (beyond trademark)
- **Documentation** -- Technical docs, API documentation, internal specs
- **Database schema design** -- The specific expression of your 12-table schema (the creative choices in structuring it)
- **Written algorithms** -- The specific code implementation of the CIS scoring engine

### What Is NOT Copyrightable

- Facts, data, and ideas (e.g., the concept of combining psychological, financial, and behavioral assessments)
- Functional elements of software (these may be patentable instead)
- Individual assessment concepts borrowed from established psychology (e.g., attachment theory, love languages)
- Standard UI patterns (e.g., a swipe gesture, a chat interface)

### Formal Copyright Registration: Yes, Do It

While copyright exists automatically, formal registration with the U.S. Copyright Office provides critical advantages:

1. **Statutory damages:** Without registration, you can only recover actual damages in infringement suits. With registration (before infringement or within 3 months of publication), you can seek statutory damages of $750-$30,000 per work infringed, or up to $150,000 per work for willful infringement.
2. **Attorney's fees:** Registration is a prerequisite for recovering attorney's fees from an infringer.
3. **Prerequisite to lawsuit:** You must register (or have a pending application) before filing an infringement lawsuit.
4. **Legal presumption:** Registration creates a legal presumption that the copyright is valid if registered within 5 years of publication.

**What to register:**
- The codebase as a single literary work (register the software as a whole)
- The assessment question bank as a literary work (register as a compilation)
- Key UI designs as visual arts works (optional but recommended)

**Cost:** $65 per work (single author, online filing via copyright.gov). With an attorney, expect $200-$500 per registration including prep.

### Work-for-Hire Considerations

If you engage contractors or employees to create any part of CompatibleIQ:

- **Employees:** Works created by employees within the scope of employment are automatically "works made for hire" -- the employer owns the copyright. But you must actually be an employer (W-2), not just calling someone an employee.
- **Contractors (1099):** This is the critical risk area. Work created by independent contractors is NOT automatically work-for-hire unless (a) both parties sign a written agreement designating it as such AND (b) the work falls into one of 9 statutory categories. Software does not cleanly fit the statutory categories, so even a "work-for-hire" clause may not be sufficient for contractor-created code.
- **Solution:** Every contractor agreement must include BOTH a work-for-hire clause AND an express IP assignment clause (belt and suspenders). See Section 6 for details.

### AI-Generated Content Considerations (Copyright)

This is addressed in detail in Section 9. The short version: code and content created with substantial human creative direction (you designing the architecture, writing prompts, selecting and modifying outputs) has the strongest claim to copyright protection. Pure AI-generated output with no human creative input may not be copyrightable under current U.S. Copyright Office guidance. Document your creative involvement thoroughly.

## 3. Patent Considerations

### Is the CIS Algorithm Patentable?

**Potentially, but with significant caveats.**

The CIS (Compatibility Index Score) algorithm -- with its weighted multi-domain scoring across 8 core quotients, 6 financial domains, and 5 shadow clusters, including hard-cap penalties (e.g., SAF >= 0.75 caps CIS at 35) -- has characteristics that could support a patent claim:

- **Novel combination:** No existing dating platform combines psychometric, financial, and behavioral risk screening into a single weighted compatibility score.
- **Specific technical implementation:** The particular weighting system (VQ 25%, AQ 22%, EQ 20%, etc.), the three-layer architecture, and the shadow profile penalty system are specific enough to potentially constitute a patentable method.

**However, there are serious hurdles:**

- **Alice Corp. v. CLS Bank (2014):** The Supreme Court ruled that abstract ideas implemented on a computer are not patentable. A scoring algorithm that simply processes data according to mathematical formulas is at high risk of being rejected as an "abstract idea."
- **To survive Alice:** You must demonstrate that the algorithm produces a "technical improvement" or "inventive concept" beyond merely automating a mental process. The shadow profile hard-cap mechanism and the three-layer progressive unlock system might qualify, but this is uncertain.
- **Prior art:** Existing compatibility algorithms (eHarmony's 29 Dimensions, OkCupid's match percentage) constitute prior art. Your patent would need to distinguish your specific approach.

### Is the 3-Layer Assessment Methodology Patentable?

The three-layer approach (psychological + financial + behavioral/shadow) as a method for compatibility assessment is more promising than a pure algorithm claim because:

- It represents a specific process with defined steps and outcomes
- The combination of psychological assessment, financial intelligence screening, and coercive behavior detection in a dating context appears novel
- The progressive unlock mechanism (assessment gates access to profiles) is a specific user experience tied to a technical implementation

**Realistic assessment:** A patent application covering the METHOD (not just the math) has a reasonable chance, particularly if framed around the safety/screening aspects (shadow profile detection of narcissistic, antisocial, borderline, abusive, and addictive patterns applied to dating/matching).

### Provisional Patent Application

**File a provisional patent application as soon as possible.** Here is why:

- **Cost:** $320 (micro-entity, which likely applies to Pivot Training as a small company with fewer than 500 employees). Compare to $1,600+ for a non-provisional.
- **What it gives you:** A filing date. The U.S. operates on a "first to file" system. A provisional establishes your priority date for 12 months.
- **What it does NOT give you:** An actual patent. You must file a non-provisional (utility) application within 12 months or the provisional expires and you lose the priority date.
- **"Patent Pending":** Once you file a provisional, you can legally mark your product as "Patent Pending," which has deterrent value against competitors.
- **What to include:** Describe the CIS scoring methodology, the three-layer assessment architecture, the shadow profile penalty system, the progressive unlock mechanism, and the specific weighting formulas. Include diagrams. It does not need claims (those come in the non-provisional). Be as detailed as possible -- you cannot add new material to the non-provisional that was not in the provisional.

### Utility vs. Design Patent

| | Utility Patent | Design Patent |
|---|---|---|
| **Protects** | How it works (method, process, algorithm) | How it looks (visual design, UI) |
| **Duration** | 20 years from filing | 15 years from grant |
| **Cost** | $5,000-$15,000+ with attorney | $1,500-$3,500 with attorney |
| **Relevance to CIQ** | HIGH -- protects the CIS methodology | LOW -- UI design changes frequently |
| **Recommendation** | Pursue via provisional first | Skip for now |

### Is a Patent Worth Pursuing for a Startup?

**Honest assessment:**

- **Provisional patent: Yes, absolutely.** At $320-$2,000 (DIY vs. attorney-assisted), the cost is minimal, and you get a filing date plus "Patent Pending" status for 12 months. This is a no-brainer.
- **Full utility patent: Maybe, but not urgently.** The full patent process costs $10,000-$20,000+ and takes 2-4 years. For a startup, this money is often better spent on product development. However, if you raise funding, investors will want to see IP protection, and a pending patent is a valuable asset.
- **Strategic value:** A patent (or even "Patent Pending" status) deters competitors, increases company valuation, and is an asset in acquisition scenarios.
- **Recommendation:** File the provisional now. Decide on the full utility application in 10-11 months based on traction and funding.

## 4. Trade Secrets

### What Qualifies as a Trade Secret in CompatibleIQ

Under the Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA) and state trade secret laws (Uniform Trade Secrets Act in most states), a trade secret is information that (a) derives economic value from not being generally known and (b) is subject to reasonable efforts to maintain its secrecy.

**CompatibleIQ trade secrets include:**

- **CIS scoring weights and formulas** -- The specific percentages (VQ 25%, AQ 22%, EQ 20%, CQ 18%, NQ 15%, LQ 10%, GQ 8%, CSQ 7%) and how they combine into a blended score
- **Shadow profile algorithms** -- The detection formulas for ACC, EMP, STB, SAF, and DEP clusters, including threshold values and hard-cap logic
- **Question bank design** -- The specific 122 questions, their mapping to quotients, and how responses are scored/weighted
- **Financial Intelligence Module scoring** -- The FMI, FSB, FPL, FIQ, FCM, and CDF scoring methodologies
- **Blended CIS calculation** -- How Layer 1 (85%), Layer 2 (15%), and Layer 3 (penalty) combine
- **Match algorithm logic** -- How CIS scores translate into profile ranking and progressive unlock thresholds
- **Proprietary data models** -- The specific data structures and relationships in your 12-table schema that reflect business logic

### How to Protect Trade Secrets

Trade secret protection requires affirmative "reasonable measures." If you fail to take reasonable steps, you lose trade secret status. Implement the following:

**1. Documentation and Identification**

- Create a "Trade Secret Register" -- a confidential internal document that lists all trade secrets, their location, and who has access
- Mark confidential documents and code files as "CONFIDENTIAL -- TRADE SECRET" where appropriate
- Document the date each trade secret was created

**2. Access Controls**

- Restrict access to the CIS scoring engine code, weighting formulas, and shadow profile algorithms to only those who need it
- Use role-based access control in your Supabase project and code repository
- Keep the scoring engine in Supabase Edge Functions (server-side) -- never expose scoring logic to the client
- Use separate repositories or protected branches for highly sensitive algorithmic code
- Enable audit logging on repository access

**3. Contractual Protection**

- **NDAs (Non-Disclosure Agreements):** Require signed NDAs from every person who accesses trade secret information -- contractors, potential co-founders, investors, advisors, beta testers with access to internal data
- **Contractor agreements:** Must include confidentiality clauses with specific reference to trade secrets (see Section 6)
- **Employee agreements:** Must include invention assignment and confidentiality provisions (see Section 6)

**4. Technical Measures**

- Keep shadow profile data completely isolated with zero client-side RLS policies (you are already doing this -- maintain it)
- Never expose scoring formulas in API responses -- only return the computed score, not the methodology
- Obfuscate or minimize any algorithmic logic that must exist on the client side
- Use environment variables and server-side configuration for scoring thresholds and weights

**5. Ongoing Practices**

- Conduct exit interviews with departing contractors/employees reminding them of confidentiality obligations
- Periodically review and update access controls
- Do not publish the specific scoring methodology in marketing materials or academic papers without careful redaction

## 5. Terms of Service & Privacy Policy

### Terms of Service -- Key Clauses

Your Terms of Service must address the unique risks of a dating/assessment platform. Required sections:

**Standard Clauses:**

- **Acceptance of Terms:** Users agree by creating an account
- **Eligibility:** Age requirement (18+), legal capacity to form a contract
- **Account creation and security:** User responsibility for credentials
- **Payment terms:** Subscription billing, refund policy, auto-renewal disclosure (required by law in many states)
- **Modification of terms:** Right to update with notice

**Platform-Specific Clauses (Critical):**

- **Assessment Data Usage:**
  - Clear disclosure of how assessment responses are used (matching, scoring, research)
  - Statement that assessment results are NOT clinical diagnoses or professional psychological evaluations
  - Disclaimer that CIS scores represent algorithmic compatibility estimates, not guarantees of relationship success
  - Right to use anonymized/aggregated assessment data for platform improvement and research

- **Shadow Profile Disclaimer:**
  - CompatibleIQ uses behavioral screening to promote user safety
  - Results are algorithmic indicators, not professional diagnoses
  - The platform is not a substitute for professional mental health evaluation
  - Limitation of liability if shadow screening fails to identify a dangerous user

- **Off-Platform Meeting Disclaimer:**
  - CompatibleIQ is not responsible for interactions that occur outside the platform
  - Users meet at their own risk
  - Recommendations for safe meeting practices (public locations, informing friends)
  - The platform does not conduct background checks (or if it does, describe the scope and limitations)

- **User Conduct:**
  - Prohibition of harassment, threats, fraud, impersonation
  - Right to suspend/terminate accounts for violations
  - Prohibition of using the platform for commercial purposes or data scraping

- **Communication Monitoring:**
  - Right to monitor in-app communications for safety and TOS compliance
  - Automated scanning for prohibited content
  - Cooperation with law enforcement when required by law
  - Retention period for chat data

- **Intellectual Property:**
  - All platform content, algorithms, and assessments are owned by Pivot Training & Development
  - Users grant a limited license to use their content (photos, bio) for platform operation
  - Users retain ownership of their personal content but grant you a license to display it

- **Limitation of Liability:**
  - CompatibleIQ is provided "as is" with no warranty of merchantability or fitness
  - No guarantee that matches will lead to compatible relationships
  - No liability for emotional distress, physical harm, financial loss, or other damages resulting from interactions with other users
  - Cap on damages (typically the amount paid by the user in the preceding 12 months)
  - Specific exclusion of liability for: user misrepresentation, off-platform conduct, assessment accuracy, compatibility predictions

- **Arbitration Clause:**
  - Mandatory binding arbitration for disputes (with exceptions for small claims court)
  - Class action waiver (upheld by the Supreme Court in AT&T v. Concepcion)
  - Specify arbitration provider (AAA or JAMS) and rules
  - Location/venue for arbitration
  - Include a 30-day informal dispute resolution period before arbitration
  - Opt-out provision (some courts require this -- allow 30 days to opt out of arbitration)

- **Termination:**
  - Right to terminate accounts with or without cause
  - Data retention/deletion upon termination
  - Surviving clauses (arbitration, limitation of liability, IP ownership)

### Privacy Policy -- Key Requirements

**Data Collection Disclosures:**

- Personal information collected (name, email, photos, demographics)
- Assessment responses and scores
- Usage data, device information, IP addresses
- Communication data (in-app messages)
- Payment information (processed through Stripe -- clarify that you do not store card numbers)
- Location data (if used for matching)

**Data Use Disclosures:**

- Matching and compatibility scoring
- Platform improvement and algorithm refinement
- Anonymized research and analytics
- Communication with users (notifications, marketing with consent)
- Safety and fraud prevention

**Data Protection Requirements:**

| Regulation | Applicability | Key Requirements |
|------------|--------------|------------------|
| **CCPA/CPRA** (California) | If you have California users (you will) | Right to know, delete, opt-out of sale; privacy notice; 12-month lookback |
| **GDPR** (EU) | If you have EU users or expand internationally | Lawful basis for processing, right to erasure, DPO requirement at scale, data processing agreements, 72-hour breach notification |
| **State privacy laws** (VA, CO, CT, etc.) | Expanding rapidly across U.S. states | Similar to CCPA with variations; check each state |
| **CAN-SPAM** | Email communications | Opt-out mechanism, physical address, honest subject lines |
| **COPPA** | If any users under 13 (should not apply) | Verify 18+ age requirement to avoid triggering |

**Sensitive Data Handling:**

- Assessment data is particularly sensitive -- it includes psychological, financial, and behavioral information
- Shadow profile data (behavioral risk indicators) is extremely sensitive and should never be disclosed to users in raw form or to third parties
- Consider whether assessment data constitutes "sensitive personal information" under CPRA (which includes data revealing psychological profiles) -- if so, additional protections apply
- Implement data minimization -- collect only what you need
- Define retention periods -- how long do you keep assessment data after account deletion?

**Specific Recommendations:**

- Hire a privacy attorney or use a reputable privacy policy generator (e.g., Termly, Iubenda) as a starting point, then have an attorney review
- Implement a cookie consent banner if required (GDPR, some state laws)
- Create a data processing addendum (DPA) for any third-party services that process user data (Supabase, Stripe, analytics tools)
- Plan for data subject access requests (DSARs) -- build the ability to export and delete a user's data

## 6. Contractor & Employee IP Assignment

### Work-for-Hire Agreements

Every contractor agreement must contain a work-for-hire provision. However, as noted in Section 2, the work-for-hire doctrine has limitations for independent contractors. Your agreements must include BOTH:

1. **Work-for-hire clause:** "Contractor agrees that all Work Product created under this Agreement constitutes a 'work made for hire' as defined by the U.S. Copyright Act, and all rights therein belong exclusively to Company."

2. **IP assignment clause (backup):** "To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as a work made for hire, Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein."

### Key Clauses for Contractor Agreements

Every contractor agreement should include:

- **Scope of work:** Clear description of deliverables
- **Work-for-hire + IP assignment:** As described above
- **Moral rights waiver:** Contractor waives any moral rights (right of attribution, right of integrity) in the work product
- **Pre-existing IP disclosure:** Contractor must disclose any pre-existing IP they will incorporate into the deliverables, and grant a perpetual license for it
- **No open-source contamination:** Contractor must not incorporate copyleft-licensed open-source code (GPL, AGPL) that could require you to open-source your proprietary code without written approval
- **Confidentiality/NDA:** Comprehensive confidentiality obligations covering trade secrets, business plans, user data, and algorithmic details
- **Non-solicitation:** Contractor agrees not to solicit your employees, contractors, or users for a specified period (typically 12-24 months)
- **Non-compete (limited):** Contractor agrees not to build a competing psychometric dating platform for a specified period. Note: non-competes for contractors are enforceable in most states but not all (California largely prohibits non-competes; a 2024/2025 landscape may have further restrictions -- consult an attorney for current law)
- **Representations and warranties:** Contractor represents the work is original and does not infringe third-party IP
- **Indemnification:** Contractor indemnifies you against IP infringement claims arising from their work

### What to Execute NOW

If you are working with (or plan to work with) any contractors, execute the following immediately:

1. **Contractor IP Assignment Agreement** -- A standalone agreement for any past or current contractors who have contributed to CompatibleIQ code, design, or content. If work has already been done without an agreement, you need a retroactive assignment.
2. **NDA** -- If not already covered in the contractor agreement
3. **Consulting/Services Agreement** -- The overarching agreement covering scope, payment, and all the clauses above

Templates are available from sources like Clerky, Cooley GO (free startup legal docs), or Y Combinator's standard CIIA (Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement). Have an attorney customize them.

## 7. Founder Protection

### Operating Agreement (If LLC)

If Pivot Training & Development is (or becomes) an LLC:

- **Single-member LLC operating agreement:** Even as a sole member, have a written operating agreement. It reinforces the corporate veil (separating personal and business liability) and documents IP ownership.
- **Key provisions:**
  - All IP created for company purposes is company property
  - Management structure and decision-making authority
  - Capital contribution records
  - Profit/loss allocation
  - Transfer restrictions (critical if adding members later)

### IP Assignment from Founder to Company

**This is one of the most important and most overlooked steps.**

If Chris Davis personally created CompatibleIQ code, assessments, and algorithms before formally assigning them to Pivot Training & Development, then Chris personally owns that IP -- not the company. This creates problems for fundraising, acquisition, and liability.

**Action required:** Execute a formal IP Assignment Agreement from Chris Davis (individually) to Pivot Training & Development (the entity), assigning all right, title, and interest in:

- The CompatibleIQ software and source code
- The CIS scoring methodology and algorithms
- The assessment question bank
- The CompatibleIQ name, logo, and tagline
- All related documentation, designs, and materials
- All patent rights (including the right to file for patents)
- All trade secrets
- All copyright interests

This should be done in exchange for consideration (even nominal -- $1.00 or the existing equity interest) and should be signed and dated. Keep the executed original in company records.

### Vesting Schedule for Future Co-Founders

If you bring on a co-founder later:

- **Standard 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff:** The co-founder earns 0% in the first year. At the 1-year mark (cliff), they vest 25%. The remaining 75% vests monthly over the next 3 years (approximately 2.08% per month).
- **Why this matters:** If a co-founder leaves after 3 months, they walk away with nothing (and no claim to your IP). Without vesting, they could claim their full equity percentage.
- **IP assignment from co-founder:** Any co-founder must sign a CIIA (Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement) assigning all IP they create for the company.
- **Founder's existing IP is separate:** Your vesting schedule as the original founder should recognize that you brought pre-existing IP. Either: (a) your shares are fully vested or partially vested from day one, or (b) the IP assignment is documented as a capital contribution that is separate from your vesting equity.

## 9. AI-Generated Content Considerations

### Current Legal Landscape (As of March 2026)

The legal status of AI-generated and AI-assisted works is evolving rapidly. Key developments:

- **U.S. Copyright Office guidance (2023-2025):** The Copyright Office has stated that works generated entirely by AI without human creative input are not copyrightable. However, works where a human exercises creative control -- selecting, arranging, and modifying AI outputs -- may contain copyrightable human authorship.
- **Thaler v. Perlmutter (2023):** Confirmed that AI-generated works without human authorship are not copyrightable. But the court did not address AI-assisted works with significant human involvement.
- **Registration practice:** The Copyright Office requires disclosure of AI-generated content in registration applications. Applicants must identify which portions are human-authored and which are AI-generated.

### How This Applies to CompatibleIQ

CompatibleIQ was built with AI assistance (Claude). The code is a product of:

- **Human creative direction:** Chris Davis designed the product architecture, defined the CIS methodology, specified the assessment framework, chose the domain weights, designed the shadow profile system, and made all substantive product decisions.
- **AI-assisted implementation:** Claude assisted with code generation, suggesting implementations based on Chris's specifications and direction.
- **Human selection and modification:** Chris selected which AI outputs to use, modified them, and integrated them into the cohesive product.

This pattern of human-directed, AI-assisted creation has the strongest claim to copyright protection.

### Best Practices for Maintaining Copyright Claims

**1. Document Human Creative Direction**

Maintain records that demonstrate your creative involvement:

- Save key conversation logs/prompts showing your design decisions and direction
- Document the product specification and architecture decisions you made before and during development
- Keep notes on where you modified, rejected, or substantially altered AI-suggested code
- Record your rationale for design choices (the CIS weighting system, shadow profile thresholds, etc.)

**2. Create a Development Log**

Maintain a simple log (even a text file in the repo) that documents:

- Date and description of major creative decisions
- What you designed/specified vs. what was AI-assisted in implementation
- Instances where you rejected AI output and wrote/rewrote code yourself
- Architecture decisions and their rationale

**3. Copyright Registration Approach**

When registering the codebase with the Copyright Office:

- Disclose the use of AI assistance in the application (required by current policy)
- Identify the human-authored elements (architecture, algorithms, creative decisions, modifications)
- Frame the registration as covering the human-authored compilation and creative expression, with AI as a tool (analogous to using an IDE with autocomplete)

**4. Strengthen Human Authorship Claims**

- Review AI-generated code and make meaningful modifications rather than accepting outputs verbatim
- Add your own comments, documentation, and structural decisions to the codebase
- The CIS scoring methodology, domain weights, shadow profile architecture, and question bank design are all human-created -- these are your strongest copyright claims regardless of how the code was implemented
- The specific expression of the 122 assessment questions (assuming you wrote/designed them) is strongly copyrightable

**5. Contractual Protection as Backup**

Regardless of copyright status, protect your work through:

- Terms of Service prohibiting reverse engineering and copying of the platform
- Trade secret protection for algorithms and scoring methods (trade secrets do not require copyright)
- Patent protection for methods (patents protect functionality regardless of copyright status)
- Contractual restrictions in all partnerships and integrations

### Key Takeaway on AI-Assisted Development

The CIS methodology, three-layer assessment architecture, shadow profile system, question bank design, and product vision are all human-originated creative works by Chris Davis. The fact that AI assisted with code implementation does not diminish the IP value -- the most protectable elements (the methodology, the specific assessment framework, the scoring system) are human-created regardless. Focus your IP protection strategy on these core innovations, and maintain documentation of your creative process.

You will benefit from two types of legal counsel:

1. **IP Attorney (Trademark + Patent):** Look for an attorney or firm specializing in intellectual property with experience in software patents and technology trademarks. Many offer flat-fee trademark filings ($1,000-$2,000 per mark) and provisional patent drafting ($1,500-$3,000).
