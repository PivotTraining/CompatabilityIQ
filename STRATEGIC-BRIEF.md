## 1. Paywall Timing Strategy

### The Core Problem

CompatibleIQ has a classic marketplace cold-start problem compounded by a completion barrier. Each user must finish 122 questions across 6 modules before they can match. If assessments are paywalled too early, the pool of completed users stays too small for meaningful matching, and the product feels broken -- not because the science is wrong, but because there is nobody on the other side.

### Phase 1: Seed the Network (Launch through 5,000 completed profiles)

**Everything is free. No paywall. No gating. Zero friction.**

- All 3 assessment layers (Core Quotients, Financial Intelligence, Shadow Profile) are free to complete
- Users receive their full personal assessment results at no cost -- this is the hook
- Discovery feed shows blurred match cards with CIS scores visible but profiles locked
- Users can see *that* they have compatible matches and *how many*, but cannot view full profiles or initiate conversation
- One free "Reveal" per week -- the user can unlock one full match profile and send a message

**Why this works:**
- The personal assessment results alone provide standalone value. Users share them. They talk about them. The assessment *is* the product-market fit test.
- Showing blurred matches with visible CIS scores creates desire without requiring payment. The user knows their 94% match exists. They just cannot reach them yet.
- The single free weekly Reveal prevents the platform from feeling like a dead end while maintaining scarcity.

**What to track before moving to Phase 2:**
- Total completed profiles (target: 5,000 minimum, ideally 10,000)
- Geographic density -- at least 3 metro areas with 500+ completed profiles each
- Assessment completion rate (target: 60%+ of users who start Module 1 finish all 6)
- Return rate -- users coming back to check for new matches at least once per week

### Phase 2: Introduce the Paywall (5,000-25,000 completed profiles)

**Founding Member Program -- the bridge between free and paid.**

Launch a "Founding Member" tier at a permanently discounted rate:

| Tier | Price | What It Includes |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Free | $0 | All assessments, personal results, 1 Reveal/week, blurred discovery feed |
| Founding Member (limited to first 1,000 paying users) | $7.99/mo (locked for life) | Unlimited Reveals, full messaging, advanced CIS breakdown, "Founding Member" badge |
| CIQ+ (standard premium) | $14.99/mo | Same as Founding Member but at full price for users who join after the cap is hit |

**Why "Founding Member" works:**
- Scarcity is real, not manufactured. There are only 1,000 spots.
- The permanent price lock rewards early adopters and creates urgency.
- The badge signals to other users that this person was here early and invested -- a subtle status marker that aligns with the ambitious, professional target audience.
- It converts the most engaged free users (the ones already using their weekly Reveal) into paying subscribers without alienating the broader base still completing assessments.

**Critical rule:** Never paywall the assessments themselves. The assessments are the acquisition engine. Charging for them kills growth.

### Phase 3: Full Monetization (25,000+ completed profiles)

Introduce the complete revenue stack:

| Revenue Stream | Price | Description |
|----------------|-------|-------------|
| Free tier | $0 | Assessments, personal results, 1 Reveal/week |
| CIQ+ Subscription | $14.99/mo | Unlimited Reveals, full messaging, advanced CIS breakdown, priority in discovery feed |
| Reveal Packs (micro-purchase) | $0.99 for 1, $2.99 for 5, $4.99 for 10 | For users who do not want a subscription but want additional Reveals |
| Pivot Growth Track | $49-$149 (one-time or per module) | Relationship coaching content tied to assessment weak spots -- e.g., if your AQ (Attachment) score is low, the Growth Track offers guided modules to build secure attachment patterns |
| Re-Assessment | $4.99 | Retake assessments after 90 days to see how scores have changed (free for CIQ+ subscribers) |
| Compatibility Deep Dive | $2.99 | Detailed report comparing you and a specific match across all quotients, with actionable relationship insights |

**Phase 3 pricing philosophy:** The free tier must always feel generous enough that a user with patience can use the app meaningfully. The paid tiers accelerate access and add depth, but the core experience -- know yourself, see that compatible people exist -- never costs money.

### Summary: The Paywall Ladder

```
ALWAYS FREE:                PAID (Phase 2+):           PREMIUM (Phase 3+):
- All assessments           - Unlimited Reveals         - Growth Track coaching
- Personal results          - Full messaging            - Deep Dive reports
- Blurred discovery feed    - Advanced CIS breakdown    - Re-Assessment
- 1 Reveal/week             - Founding Member badge     - Priority discovery
- CIS score visibility      - Read receipts             - Compatibility forecasting
```

## 2. User Acquisition Strategy

### How Tinder Built Its User Base -- And What It Teaches Us

**The Tinder playbook (2012-2013):**

Tinder did not launch nationally. It launched at one school -- the University of Southern California. The founding team, particularly Whitney Wolfe (later of Bumble), ran what became known as the "party strategy":

1. **Sorority-first seeding.** Wolfe personally visited sorority chapters at USC and pitched the app. She did not pitch it as a dating app. She pitched it as a fun game -- "see who thinks you're hot." She had sorority members download the app at the meeting before they left the room.

2. **Fraternity follow-up.** After sororities were on, she walked to the fraternity houses across the street. The pitch: "Open this app. All of these girls you know are already on it." The guys downloaded immediately.

3. **The party as a conversion event.** Tinder threw exclusive launch parties where you had to show the Tinder app on your phone to get in the door. This turned downloading the app into a social status move, not a desperate one.

4. **Campus-by-campus rollout.** After proving the model at USC, they replicated it at other colleges -- always seeding one side of the market (women) first, then leveraging that to pull in the other side.

5. **FOMO as the engine.** By making the app feel like a secret that the cool kids already knew about, Tinder bypassed the stigma that had crippled every dating app before it. You were not "desperate" for being on Tinder -- you were *in the know.*

**The key insight:** Tinder never sold dating. It sold social currency.

### Adapting the Playbook for CompatibleIQ

CompatibleIQ's target audience is different: 25-45 year old professionals, entrepreneurs, divorced parents, ambitious people. They are not on college campuses. But the underlying principles -- concentrated seeding, FOMO, social proof, and identity-based positioning -- translate directly.

### Strategy 1: The Assessment as Viral Content

**This is the single most important acquisition channel.**

The 122-question assessment produces rich, shareable personal insights across 8 quotients. This is not a dating profile. This is a personality deep-dive that people *want* to share.

**Execution:**
- After completing the assessment, generate a visual "CompatibleIQ Card" -- a beautifully designed graphic showing the user's top 3 quotients, their CIS archetype (e.g., "The Secure Challenger" or "The Analytical Nurturer"), and a prompt to take their own assessment
- The card is designed for Instagram Stories, TikTok, and LinkedIn -- square and vertical formats, dark/premium aesthetic, no cheesy dating imagery
- Include a unique referral link embedded in each card
- The card does NOT mention dating anywhere on its face. It says "What's your CompatibleIQ?" with the Pivot Training branding. This is critical -- it removes the stigma barrier entirely

**Why this works:** People share personality test results compulsively. Myers-Briggs type indicators, Enneagrams, love languages -- these go viral because they give people language for their identity. The assessment card turns every completed user into an acquisition channel.

## 3. What to Call a Compatible Match

This is a defining brand moment. The word will appear in push notifications, in-app alerts, marketing materials, and everyday conversation between users. It needs to be scientific enough to reinforce the brand's credibility, exciting enough to trigger dopamine, and short enough to fit in a notification.

### Criteria

- Works in a sentence: "You got a ___!" / "You have 3 new ___s"
- Feels exclusive and earned, not random
- Aligns with the science-first, intelligence-based brand positioning
- Distinct from competitors (Hinge: "Most Compatible"; Bumble: "Match"; Tinder: "Match")
- Easy to say out loud without feeling awkward

### The Options

**1. Resonance**
- Usage: "You have a new Resonance." / "You and Jordan have a Resonance."
- Pros: Scientific (frequency resonance, emotional resonance). Implies a deep, natural alignment that goes beyond surface attraction. Sounds beautiful. Nobody else uses it.
- Cons: Slightly abstract. Takes a beat to understand on first encounter.
- Verdict: **Top 3 candidate.** The abstraction becomes an asset -- it invites curiosity.

**2. Signal**
- Usage: "You have 3 new Signals." / "A new Signal just came in."
- Pros: Clean, modern, tech-native. Implies something cutting through noise. Works naturally in notifications. Short.
- Cons: Could feel cold or impersonal. "Signal" is already associated with the messaging app.
- Verdict: Strong functional choice but may lack emotional warmth.

**3. Sync**
- Usage: "You have a new Sync." / "You and Taylor are a Sync."
- Pros: Implies two systems aligning perfectly. Short, punchy, modern. Easy to verbalize. Works as noun and verb ("You synced with 2 new people").
- Cons: Overused in tech contexts (cloud sync, data sync). May feel utilitarian rather than special.
- Verdict: Easy to understand but may not feel premium enough.

**4. Convergence**
- Usage: "You have a new Convergence." / "A Convergence was detected."
- Pros: Scientific weight. Implies two trajectories naturally arriving at the same point. Sounds significant and intentional.
- Cons: Long (4 syllables). "Convergences" is clunky as a plural. "A Convergence was detected" sounds like a weather alert.
- Verdict: Strong concept, awkward execution.

**5. Catalyst**
- Usage: "You have a new Catalyst." / "3 Catalysts are waiting."
- Pros: Implies potential energy -- this person could change your life. Forward-looking. Exciting.
- Cons: Does not inherently communicate *mutual* compatibility. A catalyst is one-directional.
- Verdict: Great energy, imperfect semantics.

**6. Alignment**
- Usage: "You have a new Alignment." / "You and Morgan are an Alignment."
- Pros: Directly communicates what the CIS measures. Clear, accessible, warm. Works in context: "Check your Alignments."
- Cons: Somewhat generic. Could be a yoga app feature.
- Verdict: **Top 3 candidate.** Safe, clear, and effective. The fallback if bolder options feel too risky.

**7. Harmonic**
- Usage: "You have a new Harmonic." / "2 new Harmonics discovered."
- Pros: Musical metaphor -- two frequencies creating something beautiful together. Implies that compatibility is not sameness but complementarity. Elegant.
- Cons: Might feel pretentious to some users. "Harmonics" could confuse people who associate it with music theory.
- Verdict: Beautiful word. Niche risk.

**8. Spark**
- Usage: "You have a new Spark." / "3 Sparks waiting."
- Pros: Emotionally immediate. Everyone understands it. Excitement, chemistry, possibility. Short. Fun.
- Cons: Feels more like a dating app cliche than a science-first platform. Hinge's old branding used similar language. Does not differentiate.
- Verdict: Too generic for this brand.

**9. Nexus**
- Usage: "You have a new Nexus." / "A Nexus has been found."
- Pros: Means "connection point" or "linked group." Sounds important. Sci-fi adjacent in a cool way.
- Cons: Google Nexus phones. "Nexuses" as a plural is rough. May feel sterile.
- Verdict: Interesting but carries baggage.

**10. Attune**
- Usage: "You have a new Attune." / "2 Attunes are waiting."
- Pros: Psychological term (attunement is a real concept in attachment theory -- directly relevant to AQ). Implies listening, sensitivity, understanding. Unique.
- Cons: "Attune" is a verb being forced into noun territory. "Attunes" as a plural is slightly unnatural.
- Verdict: **Top 3 candidate.** The attachment theory connection makes it genuinely meaningful for this product. Grammatical awkwardness is the only weakness.

### Recommendation

**Primary: Resonance**

"You have a new Resonance" is the kind of notification that makes someone stop scrolling. It sounds important. It sounds earned. It sounds like something was *discovered*, not just algorithmically generated. It carries scientific credibility (resonance is a real physics concept) while feeling emotionally warm (emotional resonance, personal resonance).

In marketing: "Find your Resonance." In conversation: "I got a Resonance on CompatibleIQ." In the app: "3 new Resonances."

**Runner-up: Alignment** (if Resonance tests as too abstract with focus groups)
**Dark horse: Attune** (if you want to lean harder into the psychology positioning)

## 4. The Match Moment -- Sensation Design

### Design Philosophy

Most dating apps treat a match as a dopamine hit -- confetti, fireworks, a burst of color that fades in seconds. CompatibleIQ users invested 49 minutes in assessments to get here. The match moment must feel *earned*, not cheap. It should feel like opening an envelope, not pulling a slot machine lever.

The sensation should communicate: **"The science found someone. This is rare. Pay attention."**

### The Reveal Sequence (5-7 seconds total)

**Second 0-1: The Pulse**
- The screen dims slightly (5% opacity overlay)
- A single, subtle pulse animation radiates from the center of the screen -- a soft ring of the brand's accent color expanding outward once, like a sonar ping
- A gentle haptic tap (single medium impact on iOS, equivalent on Android)
- No sound yet

**Second 1-3: The Score Emergence**
- The CIS score fades in at the center of the screen: large, clean typography
- The number counts up from 0 to the actual score (e.g., 0 -> 87) over 2 seconds
- As the number climbs, the background gradually shifts to a warm gradient (cool blue at low numbers, transitioning through purple to warm gold as it climbs past 80)
- A low, warm tone plays -- think the opening note of a piano chord, not a bell or chime. Resonant. Sustained.
- Second haptic: a slow, building vibration pattern (low-medium-high) timed to the number climbing

**Second 3-5: The Card**
- The score settles at its final number and gently lifts upward on the screen
- Below it, a match card slides up from the bottom: the match's first name, age, and a single blurred photo
- The blurred photo has just enough detail to suggest a person but not identify them -- this is intentional
- Text appears: "A new Resonance has been found."
- Below the card, a soft-glow CTA button: "Reveal"

**Second 5-7: The Context**
- Small text fades in below the CTA showing 2-3 top compatibility highlights:
  - "Values: 94% aligned"
  - "Financial Intelligence: 91% aligned"
  - "Conflict Style: Complementary"
- These give the user *reason* to reveal, not just curiosity

### What Is NOT Revealed Initially

- Full photo (blurred until Reveal is used)
- Last name
- Specific location (only "12 miles away" or similar)
- Shadow Profile data (never shown to users)
- Full CIS breakdown by quotient (available after Reveal, in detail after messaging begins)

### Progressive Disclosure After Reveal

| Stage | What Unlocks |
|-------|-------------|
| Reveal used | Full photo, first name, age, city, bio, top 3 compatibility highlights |
| First message sent | Full CIS breakdown by quotient (8 scores) |
| 10 messages exchanged | Financial compatibility summary (Layer 2 overview, not raw scores) |
| Mutual "Ready to Meet" | General neighborhood/area (not exact location) |

### How Competitors Handle This (and where CompatibleIQ diverges)

**Tinder:** Instant mutual match screen with both photos side by side. Confetti. "It's a Match!" Feels celebratory but cheap. Designed for volume, not quality.

**Hinge:** "Most Compatible" badge on a profile in the feed. No special reveal sequence. Subtle but forgettable. Feels like a suggestion, not a discovery.

**Bumble:** Match notification with photo and name. Clean but unremarkable. No sense of exclusivity.

**CompatibleIQ's advantage:** The 49-minute assessment investment means users have *earned* this moment. No other app can deliver a match with the weight of "we analyzed 122 dimensions of who you are and found someone who aligns." The slow reveal sequence respects that investment.

### Notification Design (Lock Screen)

```
CompatibleIQ
A new Resonance has been found.
CIS: 87 -- Tap to discover.
```

- No photo on the lock screen notification (privacy-first)
- The CIS score is included because it creates specific, quantified curiosity -- "87 out of what? Is that good?" pulls the user in
- Clean, minimal, no emoji

### Sound Design Notes

- The match notification sound should be unique and recognizable -- think the HBO static sound or the Netflix "ta-dum." A 2-3 second audio signature that users learn to associate with "someone compatible was found."
- Suggested direction: a single low piano note that resolves into a warm chord. Analog, not digital. Human, not robotic.
- This sound should ONLY play for Resonance notifications, never for messages or other alerts. Protect its meaning.

## 5. Internal Messaging Rules

### Core Principle

CompatibleIQ messaging is designed for *getting to know someone*, not for exchanging content. The constraints are features, not limitations. They force genuine conversation and protect users from the most common vectors of harassment, catfishing, and abuse.

### Message Format Rules

**Allowed:**
- Text messages (up to 500 characters per message)
- Emoji (standard Unicode emoji set)
- Voice notes (up to 60 seconds, with transcription available)

**Not allowed:**
- Photo sharing (no camera, no gallery access in chat)
- Video sharing
- File sharing of any kind
- Link sharing (URLs are automatically stripped and replaced with "[link removed -- CompatibleIQ keeps conversations here]")
- GIFs or stickers
- Location sharing (pins, addresses, coordinates)
- Contact card sharing (phone numbers, email addresses, social media handles)

**Why no photos:** Photo sharing is the #1 vector for unsolicited explicit content. Removing it entirely eliminates the problem at the infrastructure level rather than relying on moderation to catch it after the fact. Users who want to share photos can do so after mutually agreeing to move to another platform -- but CompatibleIQ will not be the medium.

**Why no links:** Links enable phishing, spam, and redirect to content the platform cannot moderate. Removing them keeps the conversation contained and safe.

### Contact Information Detection

The messaging system must include automated detection for:
- Phone numbers (regex pattern matching for US/international formats)
- Email addresses
- Social media handles (@username patterns, "add me on [platform]")
- Physical addresses

When detected, the message is sent but the sensitive content is redacted and replaced with: "[personal info removed for your safety]"

The sender receives a gentle nudge: "CompatibleIQ removes personal contact info from messages. When you're both ready, use the 'Ready to Meet' feature to safely plan a meetup."

This is NOT a block or a punishment. It is a safety guardrail with a clear path forward.

### Safety Features

**Report:**
- Available on every message via long-press or swipe
- Report categories: Harassment, Threatening language, Spam/scam, Impersonation, Inappropriate content, Other
- Reports are tagged with the message content, conversation context (5 messages before and after), and both users' account metadata
- A user who receives 3+ reports from different users within 30 days is automatically flagged for manual review
- A user who receives a "Threatening language" report is immediately shadow-restricted (their messages are delivered with a delay while a moderator reviews)

**Block:**
- One-tap block from the conversation screen or the user's profile
- Blocking is immediate and silent -- the blocked user sees no indication they have been blocked
- Blocked users see the conversation disappear from their list (as if the other person deleted their account)
- Blocked users can never appear in the blocker's discovery feed again
- Block data is permanent and survives account deletion/recreation (tied to device fingerprint and email)

**Flag for Concern:**
- A softer option than Report -- "Something feels off but I can't pinpoint it"
- Flags are logged and contribute to a risk score on the flagged user's account
- 5+ flags from different users triggers a manual review even if no formal reports exist

**Emergency Resources:**
- A persistent but non-intrusive "Safety Resources" link in every conversation's menu
- Links to: National Domestic Violence Hotline (1-800-799-7233), Crisis Text Line (text HOME to 741741), local law enforcement (based on user's general location)
- These resources are always one tap away but never patronizing

### Moderation Approach

**Automated first pass:**
- All messages are scanned by an NLP moderation layer before delivery
- Flagged content categories: explicit sexual language (beyond flirtation), threats, hate speech, financial solicitation
- Flagged messages are held for 30 seconds while automated review runs -- in practice, users perceive no delay
- Messages that clear automated review are delivered normally
- Messages that fail automated review are held and a human moderator is alerted

**Human moderation team:**
- Target: 4-hour response time for flagged content during business hours, 12-hour maximum overnight
- Moderators can: deliver the held message, delete the held message and warn the sender, suspend the sender's messaging for 24/48/72 hours, permanently ban the account
- All moderation decisions include a brief written rationale that is logged (not shown to users)

**Proactive patterns to watch for:**
- Users who message 10+ matches within 24 hours with identical or near-identical opening messages (spam/bot behavior)
- Users who attempt to share contact info in creative ways (spelling out numbers, using letter substitution)
- Sudden escalation in message intensity (calm -> aggressive within a few messages)

## 6. Security & Legal Disclaimers

### Meeting Outside the App -- Disclaimer Language

The following disclaimer must appear at three touchpoints: (1) when a user first unlocks messaging, (2) when two users mutually activate "Ready to Meet," and (3) in the app's Terms of Service.

**In-app disclaimer (shown at "Ready to Meet" activation):**

> **A note about meeting in person**
>
> CompatibleIQ is designed to help you understand compatibility. It is not a guarantee of character, safety, or intent.
>
> When meeting someone from CompatibleIQ for the first time:
> - Choose a public place with other people around
> - Tell a friend or family member where you will be, who you are meeting, and when you expect to return
> - Arrange your own transportation to and from the meeting
> - Do not share your home address until you have established trust over multiple meetings
> - Trust your instincts -- if something feels wrong, leave
>
> CompatibleIQ's assessments measure psychological and behavioral compatibility. They do not verify identity, conduct background checks, or guarantee safety. The Compatibility Index Score (CIS) reflects self-reported assessment responses and should not be interpreted as a safety rating.
>
> If you experience threatening behavior, contact local law enforcement immediately. You can also reach the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 1-800-799-7233.

### Exchanging Personal Information -- Disclaimer Language

> **About sharing personal information**
>
> CompatibleIQ restricts the exchange of personal contact information within the app to protect your privacy and safety. When you choose to share personal information outside of CompatibleIQ, you do so at your own risk.
>
> We recommend:
> - Using a secondary phone number (Google Voice, Burner app) rather than your primary number
> - Avoiding sharing your home or work address early in a relationship
> - Being cautious with social media connections -- they can reveal your location, workplace, and daily routine
> - Never sharing financial information, government IDs, or login credentials with someone you met online

### Data Protection Statement

> **How CompatibleIQ protects your data**
>
> - All assessment responses are encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
> - Shadow Profile data (Layer 3 behavioral screening) is stored in an isolated database partition with zero client-side access. No user -- including the assessed individual -- can view raw Shadow Profile scores through the app interface. This data is used exclusively by the CIS scoring engine.
> - Your exact location is never stored. CompatibleIQ uses approximate geolocation (city-level) for matching purposes only.
> - We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data or assessment results with third parties for marketing purposes.
> - You may request complete deletion of your account and all associated data at any time. Deletion is permanent and irreversible, completed within 30 days per applicable regulations.
> - Assessment responses are anonymized and may be used in aggregate (never individually identifiable) to improve the CIS algorithm.

### Terms of Service -- Safety Highlights

Key provisions to include in the full ToS:

1. **No identity verification disclaimer:** "CompatibleIQ does not conduct identity verification, background checks, criminal record checks, or sex offender registry checks on its users. You are solely responsible for taking appropriate precautions when interacting with other users."

2. **Assessment accuracy disclaimer:** "The Compatibility Index Score and all assessment results are based on self-reported responses and proprietary algorithms. They are intended for informational and entertainment purposes and should not be relied upon as psychological diagnoses, safety assessments, or guarantees of relationship success."

3. **Shadow Profile ethics clause:** "CompatibleIQ employs behavioral screening algorithms designed to identify potentially harmful relationship patterns. Users who score above safety thresholds on these screens may have their matching capabilities restricted. CompatibleIQ reserves the right to limit or terminate access based on behavioral screening results without disclosing specific scores or thresholds."

4. **Minimum age:** 18 years. Verified at account creation via date of birth with periodic re-verification.

5. **One account per person:** Multi-accounting results in permanent ban of all associated accounts.

6. **No commercial use:** The platform may not be used for solicitation, advertising, promotion, or any commercial purpose.

## 7. Location Strategy

### Core Principles

1. **Privacy is non-negotiable.** No user's exact location is ever visible to another user, stored in precise form, or transmitted to the client application.
2. **Location enhances matching but does not define it.** Users can browse matches nationwide but are shown proximity indicators to help prioritize.
3. **Opt-in only.** Location services are never required. Users who decline location access can manually set their city.

### Location Services Opt-In Flow

**First prompt (after assessment completion, before entering discovery feed):**

> **Help us find Resonances near you**
>
> CompatibleIQ uses your general location (city-level only) to show you compatible people nearby. We never store or share your exact address.
>
> [Allow Location] [Set City Manually] [Skip for Now]

**If "Allow Location" is selected:**
- Request iOS/Android location permission (While Using the App only -- never "Always")
- Convert precise coordinates to a city/metro area on-device before transmitting to the server
- The server receives and stores only: city name, state, and a randomized coordinate within a 5-mile radius of the actual location
- This randomized coordinate is what is used for distance calculations

**If "Set City Manually" is selected:**
- User searches for and selects their city from a list
- Distance calculations use the city center as the reference point
- The profile displays the city name but no distance indicator (since distance would be inaccurate)

**If "Skip for Now" is selected:**
- The user enters the discovery feed with no location filtering
- All matches are shown regardless of geography
- A persistent but dismissible banner at the top: "Set your location to see who's nearby"

### Distance Display Logic

**What users see:**
- "In [City Name]" -- for matches in the same metro area
- "X miles away" -- rounded to the nearest 5 miles (never exact). Minimum displayed distance: 5 miles. So a match 2 miles away still shows "5 miles away"
- "Nearby" -- for matches within 25 miles (alternative to exact distance for extra privacy)

**What users never see:**
- Exact distance in miles or kilometers
- The other user's neighborhood, zip code, or street
- Real-time location updates
- Location history or movement patterns

### Nationwide Browsing + Local Prioritization

The discovery feed uses a weighted ranking system:

```
Feed Rank = CIS Score (70%) + Proximity Bonus (20%) + Activity Recency (10%)
```

- Proximity Bonus: matches within 25 miles get the full 20% bonus, scaling linearly down to 0% at 250+ miles
- This means a 92 CIS match 200 miles away will often rank higher than a 75 CIS match 5 miles away -- compatibility comes first
- Users can apply a distance filter (25 / 50 / 100 / 250 / Anywhere) to override this default

### Location Updates

- Location is refreshed only when the user opens the discovery feed (not in background)
- If a user has not opened the app in 14+ days, their location data is staled and they appear with "Location unavailable" rather than a potentially outdated city
- Users can update their city manually at any time (for travel, relocation, etc.)

## 8. Notification Strategy

### Philosophy

Every notification CompatibleIQ sends must pass a single test: **"Would I be glad I saw this?"** If the answer is not a clear yes, do not send it.

Dating app notification fatigue is real. Users who feel badgered uninstall. Users who feel forgotten churn. The goal is to be the app that communicates with intention -- every notification is meaningful, and silence between notifications is comfortable.

### Notification Channels

| Channel | Use Case | Opt-In Default |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Push notification | Time-sensitive: new Resonance, new message, safety alert | ON |
| In-app notification | Activity feed: who viewed your profile, Reveal usage, assessment reminders | ON (always on, this is in-app only) |
| Email | Weekly digest, account security, Founding Member announcements | ON |
| SMS | Two-factor authentication only | ON (required for 2FA) |

**SMS is never used for marketing or engagement.** This is a deliberate choice. SMS marketing for a dating app feels invasive and cheap. It undermines the premium positioning.

### Push Notification Inventory

**Tier 1: Always send (cannot be individually disabled, only "all push" can be turned off)**

| Trigger | Notification | Timing |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| New Resonance found | "A new Resonance has been found. CIS: [score]." | Immediately, but respect Quiet Hours |
| New message received | "[First Name] sent you a message." | Immediately, but batch if 3+ messages arrive within 5 minutes |
| Safety alert | "Your account security requires attention." | Immediately, ignore Quiet Hours |

**Tier 2: Default ON, user can disable**

| Trigger | Notification | Timing |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Match viewed your profile | "[First Name] viewed your profile." | Batched: delivered at most once per 4 hours |
| Incomplete assessment reminder | "You're [X]% through your assessment. Pick up where you left off." | 24 hours after last activity, then 72 hours, then 7 days. Max 3 reminders total. |
| Re-assessment available | "It's been 90 days. Your perspectives may have evolved. Retake your assessment." | Once, at the 90-day mark |
| Mutual "Ready to Meet" | "[First Name] is also ready to meet. Plan your first conversation." | Immediately |

**Tier 3: Default OFF, user can enable**

| Trigger | Notification | Timing |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| New user in your area | "Someone new in [City] just completed their assessment." | At most once per week |
| CIS algorithm update | "We've refined our compatibility engine. Check for new Resonances." | Only on major updates, at most quarterly |

### Frequency Caps

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Maximum push notifications per day | 5 (excluding safety alerts) |
| Maximum push notifications per hour | 2 |
| Quiet Hours | 10:00 PM - 8:00 AM local time (user-adjustable) |
| Quiet Hours exception | Direct messages from an active conversation (user has sent a message in the last 2 hours) |
| Post-uninstall email | 1 email, 7 days after uninstall. "We saved your assessment results." Then silence. |
| Re-engagement after 30 days inactive | 1 push (if app still installed) + 1 email. Then silence until user returns. |

### Email Strategy

**Weekly Digest (Tuesdays at 10:00 AM local time):**
- Subject line rotation:
  - "Your week on CompatibleIQ: [X] new Resonances"
  - "[First Name], here's what's new in your compatibility feed"
  - "Your CIS Weekly: [X] matches, [Y] messages"
- Content: number of new Resonances, number of unread messages, one anonymized compatibility insight ("This week, your highest-scoring match was 94% aligned on Values"), CTA to open the app
- If the user had zero activity that week: skip the digest entirely. Do not send an email that says "Nothing happened." Silence is better than emptiness.

**Transactional Emails:**
- Account creation confirmation
- Password reset
- Subscription confirmation/cancellation
- Payment receipt
- Account deletion confirmation

**Lifecycle Emails (automated sequence for new users):**

| Day | Email | Purpose |
|-----|-------|---------|
| Day 0 | "Welcome to CompatibleIQ. Here's what to expect." | Set expectations for the assessment journey |
| Day 1 | "The science behind your compatibility score" | Educate on CIS methodology, build trust |
| Day 3 (if assessment incomplete) | "You're [X]% through. Here's what Module [Y] measures." | Re-engage with curiosity, not pressure |
| Day 7 (if assessment complete) | "Your results are in. Here's what they mean." | Deep dive into their personal results, drive app opens |
| Day 14 | "How CompatibleIQ matching works" | Explain the discovery feed, Reveals, and Resonances |
| Day 30 | "Your first month: [X] Resonances, [Y] messages" | Milestone celebration, conversion prompt for CIQ+ |

### Smart Notification Triggers

Beyond standard event-based notifications, implement these behavioral triggers:

1. **The Comeback Ping:** If a user opens the app, scrolls the discovery feed, but does not use a Reveal or send a message for 3 consecutive sessions -- send a notification 24 hours after the third session: "Your top Resonance this week scored [X]. Worth a look?"

2. **The Conversation Nudge:** If two users exchanged 5+ messages and then neither has messaged in 48 hours -- send both users: "[First Name] might be waiting to hear from you." (Only trigger once per conversation stall.)

3. **The Completion Pull:** If a user completed the first 3 modules but stalled -- wait 48 hours, then: "You've completed the hardest part. Modules 4-6 take about 15 minutes total." (Slightly understate the time to reduce perceived friction.)

4. **The Social Proof Nudge:** If 3+ new users in the user's city completed assessments in the past week -- "Your area is growing. [X] new people in [City] completed their assessments this week."

### Opt-Out Design

- Settings screen: clear, grouped toggles for each notification category
- Each push notification includes a "..." menu with "Turn off this type of notification" -- one tap, no settings navigation required
- Unsubscribe from email: one-click, instant, no "are you sure?" page
- If a user disables ALL push notifications, show a single in-app message next time they open the app: "You've turned off all notifications. You'll still see new Resonances and messages when you open the app." Then never mention it again.
